HOW TO PLAY: GUESS THE CELEBRITY

Guess the Celebrity is a fast, simple trivia game where you get three clues and try to name the famous person, group, creator, athlete, actor, singer, or icon.

GOAL
Score as many points as possible before you run out of lives.

HOW EACH ROUND WORKS
1. In Normal Mode, all 3 clues are visible right away.
2. Guess the celebrity by typing their name and pressing Guess.
3. You can also tap one of the quick-answer buttons.
4. In Hard Mode, you start with 1 clue and reveal more clues when needed.
5. If you are correct, you earn points and move to the next round.
6. If you are wrong or the timer runs out, you lose a life.

SCORING
Correct answers give you points.
Faster answers give you bonus points.
Long streaks give you bigger bonuses.
Revealing extra hints lowers the points you can earn that round.
Harder later rounds give a small difficulty bonus.
Your best score is saved on the same device.

LIVES AND SKIPS
You start with 3 lives.
Wrong answers and time-outs cost 1 life.
You start with 3 skips.
Skipping reveals the answer and moves you to a new round, but it resets your streak.

MODES
Chill Mode gives you more time and is best for casual play.
Blitz Mode gives you less time and higher pressure.
Hard Mode hides later clues until you reveal them.
In Hard Mode, quick-answer buttons unlock after clue 2.
Daily Mix gives everyone the same seeded run for the day.
Shuffle Round instantly swaps in a fresh celebrity.

DIFFICULTY
The game gets harder the longer you survive.
The timer gets shorter.
The answer grid adds more choices.
Later rounds may hide the celebrity category.

SOUNDS AND EFFECTS
Buttons have click sounds.
Correct answers play a success sound and confetti.
Wrong answers play a miss sound.
You can mute sounds with the Sound button.
You can turn the background music on and off with the Music button.

WHY IT STAYS FRESH
The game uses a curated celebrity clue engine with 100,000+ possible clue mixes, so rounds can feel different even when familiar names come back.
Celebrities do not repeat in the same run until the full pool has been used.

TIP
Partial names often work. If the answer is Taylor Swift, typing Taylor is enough.
